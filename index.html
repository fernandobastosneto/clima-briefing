Critical Minerals Rush: Brazil's Controversial Bill Meets Rare Earth Reality

Sources: Manchetes Socioambientais, ClimaInfo, Climate Home News

Brazil's lower house approved the text of PL 2.780/2024, creating a National Policy for Critical and Strategic Minerals with a R$5 billion credit fund and a state-backed guarantee facility of up to R$2 billion. Environmental and Indigenous rights organizations warn the bill ignores environmental safeguards and Indigenous community protections. ClimaInfo notes the vote came just one day before Presidents Lula and Trump met to discuss rare earth exploitation. In parallel, Climate Home News profiles startups racing to build EV motors without rare earth elements — a bid to break China's near-monopoly on the global supply chain. Together, these stories paint a picture of a world scrambling for critical minerals while grappling with the environmental and geopolitical costs of extraction.
