UK Consults on Amending HFC Phasedown Schedule

Sources: GOV.UK

The UK government is seeking views on reforming the hydrofluorocarbon (HFC) phasedown schedule in Great Britain, publishing a public statement with an update on the reform process. HFCs are potent greenhouse gases with warming potentials thousands of times greater than CO₂, and their phasedown under the Kigali Amendment is a cornerstone of near-term climate mitigation. The consultation signals the UK's intent to accelerate the transition toward climate-friendly refrigerants.

WHO Must Prioritize Workers as Climate Risks Intensify

Sources: Inside Climate News

Advocates are urging the World Health Organization to restore occupational safety and health programs after Trump-era funding cuts led the agency to retreat from the field — just as climate change makes workplaces far more dangerous. Nearly 3 million people die annually from job-related accidents or exposures, and rising heat and toxic wildfire smoke are compounding the risks for millions of outdoor and essential workers worldwide. The call comes ahead of a key WHO leadership meeting next week where advocates hope to see worker protections elevated on the global health agenda.

US Weighs Eliminating the National Center for Atmospheric Research

Sources: Inside Climate News

A proposal to eliminate the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) — one of the world's premier climate science institutions — is raising alarms across the research community. NCAR provides foundational climate modeling, weather prediction, and atmospheric data that underpin both US and global climate science. Its loss would set back understanding of climate dynamics at precisely the moment when extreme weather events demand better forecasting and attribution.
