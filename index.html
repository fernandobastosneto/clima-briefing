Canada Sets C$140/t Carbon Price by 2040, Plans Trillion-Dollar Grid Expansion

Sources: Carbon Pulse, Bloomberg Green

Canada and Alberta have agreed on a headline carbon price of C$140 per tonne by 2040 for the province's TIER (Technology Innovation and Emissions Reduction Regulation) market, reinforcing the federal benchmark. Prime Minister Mark Carney simultaneously published a strategy to double Canada's electricity generation by 2050, loosening clean electricity rules to accommodate more natural gas — with total costs expected to top C$1 trillion. Carney acknowledged the tension: "We'll update our climate plans and our emission reduction targets in due course."

D.C. Circuit Court Hears First Challenge to Trump Emergency Orders Keeping Coal Plants Open

Sources: Inside Climate News

The D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals is hearing the first legal challenge to Trump administration orders that block the retirement of aging coal and oil plants. Advocates argue keeping the plants running is costly to consumers and the environment, while the administration asserts it alone has the power to declare energy emergencies. The case could set a major precedent for federal authority over fossil fuel phaseouts and grid decarbonization.

El Niño Has 82% Chance of Developing by July, Intensity Uncertain

Sources: ClimaInfo

The U.S. Climate Prediction Center now gives an 82% probability of El Niño developing between May and July, with a 96% chance it persists through the Northern Hemisphere winter. Scientists diverge on intensity: some models give a greater than 60% chance of a "strong" or "very strong" event by year-end, while others cap the probability below 40%. A powerful El Niño could drive new global temperature records and intensify extreme weather events.

Voluntary Carbon Removal Market Surpasses 1 Million Tonnes in April

Sources: Carbon Pulse

The voluntary durable carbon dioxide removal (CDR) market reached a milestone with over 1.14 million tonnes contracted in April, with JPMorgan and Boeing adding to existing portfolios. A dedicated CDR registry saw record monthly issuances and retirements. Separately, the first real-world trial of olivine-based marine CDR reported no adverse ecological impacts, clearing a key hurdle for ocean-based carbon removal — while biochar producers emphasized that delivery certainty and long-term offtake agreements are essential for scaling.
