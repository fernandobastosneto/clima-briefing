Brazil Approves Critical Minerals Law — Environmental & Indigenous Concerns Multiply

Sources: ClimaInfo, Manchetes Socioambientais, Reset News, Climate Home News

Brazil's Câmara dos Deputados approved the text-base of the Política Nacional de Minerais Críticos e Estratégicos (PL 2.780/2024), creating a R$5 billion tax credit program and a R$2 billion federal guarantee fund to stimulate rare earth processing. The vote came one day before Presidents Lula and Trump were set to discuss rare earth cooperation. But organizations across the environmental and Indigenous rights spectrum are sounding alarms: the framework deepens an extractive model without guaranteeing public benefits, ignores safeguards for Indigenous territories, and lacks environmental impact provisions. Manchetes Socioambientais quotes critics calling it a direct threat to communities and ecosystems. Climate Home News provides global context: startups from the UK to India are racing to build EV motors that eliminate rare earths entirely, proving alternatives exist — but are being outpaced by policy.
